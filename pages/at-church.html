Today's Study

Sunday, July 12, 2026 · 11:49 AM CDT

Scripture Focus: 1 Thessalonians 5:12–14

1. Scripture First (KJV)

12 And we beseech you, brethren, to know them which labour among you, and are over you in the Lord, and admonish you; 13 And to esteem them very highly in love for their work's sake. And be at peace among yourselves. 14 Now we exhort you, brethren, warn them that are unruly, comfort the feebleminded, support the weak, be patient toward all men.

2. Context Matters

Who is speaking: The Apostle Paul, Silas, and Timothy.
Who the audience is: The young, persecuted congregation in Thessalonica.

After preparing the church for the certainty of Christ's return (v. 1–11), Paul provides a rapid-fire set of instructions on how the local church family must function day-to-day while waiting. He addresses internal order, leadership dynamics, and interpersonal responsibilities to ensure the church remains spiritually healthy and unified.

3. Key Characters

The Brethren: The whole church body tasked with mutual care.
Those who labor / are over you: The local elders and spiritual leaders guiding the flock.
The Unruly, Feebleminded, and Weak: Distinct groups within the congregation requiring specific, intentional ministry.

4. Clear Summary

Paul outlines a two-pronged strategy for a healthy church community. First, verses 12–13 define the congregation's relationship with its spiritual leaders: they are to recognize their hard work, hold them in high esteem, and live in harmony. Second, verse 14 shifts the heavy lifting of ministry to the entire church. Believers are commanded to confront the disorderly, console the discouraged, physically hold up the weak, and show endless patience to everyone.

5. Original Language Insight

"Know" (v. 12 — Eidō): This goes beyond recognizing a face; it means to appreciate, value, and respect the weight of the office and effort of those leading.
"Unruly" (v. 14 — Ataktos): A military term meaning "out of rank." It applies to someone breaking formation or shirking their responsibilities.
"Feebleminded" (v. 14 — Oligopsychos): Literally "small-souled" or faint-hearted. It describes those overwhelmed by grief, fear, or anxiety who need a gentle reminder of God's promises.

6. Scripture Interprets Scripture

On Appreciating Leaders: Compare v. 12–13 with 1 Timothy 5:17, "Let the elders that rule well be counted worthy of double honour, especially they who labour in the word and doctrine."
On Supporting the Weak: Compare v. 14 with Romans 14:1, "Him that is weak in the faith receive ye..."
On Overcoming Retaliation: Compare the peaceful mandate here with Proverbs 20:22, "Say not thou, I will recompense evil; but wait on the Lord, and he shall save thee."

7. Application to Life

Owning the Ministry: Verse 14 makes it clear that pastoral care belongs to the pews, not just the pulpit. We are all called to look out for our spiritual family members.
Discerning the Need: We must learn to distinguish between a rebellious heart that needs a firm warning and a broken spirit that needs gentle comfort.
The Calling to Patience: Extending patience "toward all men" means we do not write people off when they stumble or act difficult.
